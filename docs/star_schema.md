# Retail Analytics Star Schema

## Purpose

The star schema organizes retail data around the central sales business process. It is designed to support analytical questions about revenue, quantity, products, customers, stores, and time.

The model is intentionally flexible. It does not assume that every source dataset contains every enterprise attribute. Missing or unavailable attributes can be added later as nullable fields, enrichment tables, or new dimensions.

## Schema Overview

```text
warehouse.dim_date
        |
        |
warehouse.dim_product ---- warehouse.fact_sales ---- warehouse.dim_customer
        |
        |
warehouse.dim_store
```

Core tables:

- `warehouse.fact_sales`
- `warehouse.dim_date`
- `warehouse.dim_product`
- `warehouse.dim_customer`
- `warehouse.dim_store`

## Grain

The recommended grain for `warehouse.fact_sales` is one sales observation at the lowest reliable level available from the source.

Depending on source availability, this may represent:

- One invoice line from the online retail dataset.
- One store, department, and week sales observation from the Walmart dataset.

Because the project uses more than one retail dataset, the warehouse should preserve source lineage and avoid forcing incompatible fields into a single narrow design too early. A practical implementation can use nullable foreign keys and source metadata while maintaining a consistent analytical interface.

## Fact Table

### `warehouse.fact_sales`

Purpose:

Stores measurable retail sales events or sales observations. This table is the central table used for KPI reporting, trend analysis, forecasting, and downstream data marts.

Business role:

- Measure sales performance.
- Support trend analysis by date.
- Compare performance across products, customers, and stores.
- Provide a foundation for BI dashboards and ML features.

Primary key:

- `sales_key`: surrogate primary key generated by the warehouse.

Foreign keys:

- `date_key` references `warehouse.dim_date(date_key)`.
- `product_key` references `warehouse.dim_product(product_key)`.
- `customer_key` references `warehouse.dim_customer(customer_key)`.
- `store_key` references `warehouse.dim_store(store_key)`.

Recommended descriptive fields:

- `source_system`
- `source_record_id`
- `invoice_no`
- `is_holiday`

Expected metrics:

- `quantity`
- `unit_price`
- `gross_sales_amount`
- `weekly_sales_amount`
- `sales_amount`
- `transaction_count`

Notes:

- `sales_amount` should be the standard reporting metric where source logic is consistent.
- Source-specific measures, such as Walmart weekly sales, can be retained with clear naming.
- Additive metrics should be preferred for dashboard performance and simple aggregation.

## Dimension Tables

### `warehouse.dim_date`

Purpose:

Provides a shared calendar dimension for transaction dates, weekly sales dates, reporting periods, and time-series analysis.

Business role:

- Enables consistent reporting by day, week, month, quarter, and year.
- Supports holiday and seasonality analysis.
- Provides time attributes for BI filtering and ML features.

Primary key:

- `date_key`: integer key, commonly formatted as `YYYYMMDD`.

Expected attributes:

- `date`
- `day_of_week`
- `day_name`
- `week_of_year`
- `month`
- `month_name`
- `quarter`
- `year`
- `is_weekend`
- `is_holiday`

Related fact foreign key:

- `warehouse.fact_sales.date_key`

Expected analytical use:

- Monthly revenue trend.
- Weekly sales performance.
- Holiday versus non-holiday performance.
- Year-over-year comparison when data coverage allows.

### `warehouse.dim_product`

Purpose:

Stores product or department-level descriptors used to analyze what was sold.

Business role:

- Supports product performance reporting.
- Enables category or department-level sales analysis when attributes are available.
- Connects transaction records to product identifiers from source systems.

Primary key:

- `product_key`: surrogate primary key generated by the warehouse.

Expected natural keys:

- `stock_code` for product-level online retail data.
- `dept` for department-level Walmart data.

Expected attributes:

- `product_code`
- `product_name`
- `department_code`
- `category`
- `source_system`

Related fact foreign key:

- `warehouse.fact_sales.product_key`

Expected analytical use:

- Top-selling products.
- Revenue by product or department.
- Product contribution to total sales.
- Product mix analysis.

### `warehouse.dim_customer`

Purpose:

Stores customer descriptors where customer-level information is available.

Business role:

- Supports customer behavior analysis.
- Enables repeat purchase and customer value analysis.
- Preserves a flexible structure for future customer enrichment.

Primary key:

- `customer_key`: surrogate primary key generated by the warehouse.

Expected natural keys:

- `customer_id` where available from the source.

Expected attributes:

- `customer_id`
- `country`
- `source_system`

Related fact foreign key:

- `warehouse.fact_sales.customer_key`

Expected analytical use:

- Revenue by customer.
- Customer purchase frequency.
- Sales by country.
- Customer retention and segmentation when sufficient data exists.

Notes:

- Not every dataset contains customer-level data.
- For source records without customers, use a controlled unknown customer record rather than breaking foreign key consistency.

### `warehouse.dim_store`

Purpose:

Stores store-level descriptors for store-based retail analysis.

Business role:

- Supports store performance reporting.
- Enables comparison by store type, size, or geography when available.
- Provides the main location dimension for Walmart-style sales observations.

Primary key:

- `store_key`: surrogate primary key generated by the warehouse.

Expected natural keys:

- `store_id` where available from the source.

Expected attributes:

- `store_id`
- `store_type`
- `store_size`
- `country`
- `region`
- `source_system`

Related fact foreign key:

- `warehouse.fact_sales.store_key`

Expected analytical use:

- Sales by store.
- Sales by store type.
- Store productivity by size.
- Holiday impact by store.

Notes:

- Online retail data may not contain a physical store identifier.
- Use an unknown or online channel store record when a source does not provide store-level detail.

## Key Design Principles

- Use surrogate keys for warehouse joins.
- Keep source natural keys as descriptive attributes.
- Preserve `source_system` to support multiple datasets.
- Use controlled unknown dimension records for missing dimensional relationships.
- Keep facts numeric and additive wherever possible.
- Build dashboard-specific aggregations in `marts`, not in the core fact table.
- Avoid hard-coding assumptions that only fit one Kaggle dataset.

## Recommended Marts

The star schema can support these downstream marts:

- `marts.sales_overview`
- `marts.product_performance`
- `marts.customer_performance`
- `marts.store_performance`
- `marts.time_series_sales`
- `marts.forecasting_features`
